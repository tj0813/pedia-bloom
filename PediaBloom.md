Build a production-grade educational encyclopedia app for children about Indonesia.

App concept:
Create a complete, beautiful, safe, interactive encyclopedia app for children aged 5–13 years old. The app teaches children about Indonesia in a fun, visual, story-driven, age-appropriate, and factual way. The app must become a general knowledge platform about Indonesia covering geography, history, culture, nature, science, society, economy, heroes, food, arts, technology, environment, and civic values.

The app name can be:
Pedia Bloom v1

Main goals:
1. Help Indonesian children understand their country deeply.
2. Make learning feel like adventure, exploration, games, stories, and discovery.
3. Provide structured encyclopedia content by age level.
4. Support parents with progress reports.
5. Support teachers with topic-based learning.
6. Make children proud of Indonesia while staying factual, inclusive, and modern.
7. Avoid boring textbook style.
8. Use interactive maps, characters, quizzes, badges, voice narration, and mini-games.

Target users:
- Children age 5–7: simple visual learning, audio narration, short facts, big images.
- Children age 8–10: deeper explanation, quizzes, comparison, simple timelines.
- Children age 11–13: richer encyclopedia articles, critical thinking, projects, maps, and reasoning.
- Parents: monitor progress, strengths, interests, and recommended next topics.
- Teachers: use topics for classroom learning.

Design style:
- Child-friendly, colorful, clean, modern, premium.
- Inspired by high-quality education apps, storybook adventure, Disney/Pixar-level warmth, but original.
- Use cute Indonesian-themed mascots, such as Garuda kid explorer, orangutan guide, Komodo friend, sea turtle navigator, or smart owl.
- Use large cards, map-based navigation, playful icons, animated transitions, and clear hierarchy.
- Avoid clutter.
- Must work beautifully on mobile, tablet 7 inch, tablet 11 inch, and desktop.

Content rules:
- All content must be factual, neutral, inclusive, and age-appropriate.
- Use simple Indonesian language for kids.
- Provide optional English vocabulary for selected terms.
- Avoid stereotypes about regions, tribes, religions, or cultures.
- Do not present myths or folklore as historical facts.
- Separate “cerita rakyat”, “sejarah”, and “fakta ilmiah”.
- Always include source/reference field in CMS.
- For dynamic data like population, number of provinces, government leaders, economy, and infrastructure, mark as “needs regular update”.
- Every article must have:
  1. Short version for age 5–7
  2. Medium version for age 8–10
  3. Advanced version for age 11–13
  4. Quiz
  5. Fun fact
  6. Illustration direction
